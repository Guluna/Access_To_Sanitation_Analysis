Analysis of Access to Basic Sanitation Services By Country and Income Level (2000-2022)

Global Average for Basic Sanitation (Dropdown="Countries")

  • Increase from 68% to 79% from year 2000 to 2022 indicates a positive global trend in improving sanitation infrastructure and awareness.

Trend Across Income Groups (Dropdown="Income Groups")

  • Positive trend across all income groups (low-, lower-middle, upper-middle, and high-income).
  • Largest improvement in middle-income countries (both upper and lower).
  • High income countries have already achieved almost universal access, hence there is not much room for further improvement.
  • Despite lower initial values, a lower increase observed over time in low-income countries compared to middle-income countries.

Countries with Decline (Dropdown="Countries", Filter By="Canada, American Samoa, CAR, Congo, Georgia, Zimbabwe")

  • Heterogeneity exists across countries within each income group.
  • Decline observed over the years in some countries within each income group. For example,
    • High-income countries like Canada and American Samoa.
    • Low-income countries like CAR and Congo.
    • Middle-income (lower and upper) countries like Georgia and Zimbabwe.

Conclusion

  • Average increase over time hides variation across countries when looking at the aggregated data.
